# 🌮 Skinnytaste Chicken Quesadillas

A lighter, healthier take on classic chicken quesadillas. Packed with lean shredded chicken, sautéed peppers and onions, and gooey melted cheese, this quick meal delivers all the comfort food flavor with a fraction of the calories.

* **Prep Time:** 15 mins
* **Cook Time:** 15 mins
* **Total Time:** 30 mins
* **Servings:** 4 quesadillas

---

## 📋 Ingredients

### For the Chicken & Vegetable Filling
* **2 large boneless, skinless chicken breasts** (approx. 1 lb)
* **1 tbsp olive oil**
* **1 medium bell pepper** (any color), thinly sliced
* **½ large yellow onion**, thinly sliced
* **1 tbsp low-sodium taco seasoning** (or fajita seasoning)
* **¼ cup water**
* **Salt and black pepper**, to taste

### For Assembly
* **4 large low-carb / high-fiber tortillas** (such as Mission Carb Balance or Tumaro's)
* **1 ½ cups reduced-fat shredded Mexican blend cheese** (or Monterey Jack)
* **Olive oil spray** (or cooking spray)

### Optional Toppings
* **Pico de gallo or fresh salsa**
* **Plain nonfat Greek yogurt** (a high-protein substitute for sour cream)
* **Fresh cilantro & lime wedges**

---

## 🍳 Cooking Directions

### 1. Cook and Shred the Chicken
1. Season both sides of the chicken breasts with a pinch of salt and black pepper.
2. Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and coat lightly with cooking spray.
3. Add the chicken and cook for **6 to 7 minutes per side**, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
4. Remove the chicken from the skillet, let it rest for a few minutes, then shred it using two forks or dice it into bite-sized cubes. Set aside.

### 2. Sauté the Vegetables
1. In the same skillet, add **1 tablespoon of olive oil** over medium heat.
2. Toss in the sliced **bell peppers** and **onions**. Sauté for 4 to 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp and slightly caramelized.
3. Return the shredded chicken to the skillet with the vegetables.
4. Sprinkle the **taco seasoning** over the mixture and pour in **¼ cup of water**. 
5. Stir well and simmer for 2 to 3 minutes until most of the liquid has evaporated and the filling is coated evenly. Remove from heat.

### 3. Assemble the Quesadillas
1. Lay the tortillas flat on a clean work surface. 
2. Sprinkle about **3 tablespoons of cheese** over just one half of each tortilla.
3. Divide the chicken and vegetable filling equally among the four tortillas, layering it right on top of the cheese.
4. Top the filling with the remaining cheese (about 3 tablespoons per tortilla) to ensure a secure melt on both sides.
5. Fold the empty half of the tortilla over the filling to create a half-moon shape.

### 4. Crisp and Serve
1. Wipe the skillet clean and return it to medium heat, coating it lightly with cooking spray or oil mist.
2. Place the folded quesadillas in the skillet (work in batches if your pan isn't large enough).
3. Cook for **2 to 3 minutes per side**, pressing down gently with a spatula, until the tortillas are golden brown and crispy and the cheese is fully melted.
4. Remove from heat, let rest for 1 minute, and slice into triangles. Serve warm with your favorite toppings!

---

## 💡 Recipe Notes & Tips
* **Rotisserie Chicken Shortcut:** To make this an even faster weeknight meal, substitute the chicken breasts with 1 ½ cups of shredded store-bought rotisserie chicken breast. Skip step 1 and add the chicken directly into the pan with the cooked peppers and seasoning to warm through.
* **Oven-Baked Method:** If cooking for a crowd, place the assembled quesadillas on a large baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Spray the tops lightly with oil and bake at **400°F (205°C) for 10-12 minutes**, flipping halfway through, until crisp.
